# Israeli Consumer Protection Law — E-Commerce Requirements

## Overview
The Consumer Protection Law, 5741-1981 (Chok Hagnat HaTzarchan) is Israel's primary consumer
protection legislation. Chapter D1 specifically governs distance selling (mecher merachok),
which covers all e-commerce transactions.

## Distance Selling Rules (Mecher Merachok)

### Definition
A distance transaction is any sale conducted without the buyer and seller being physically
present together — including online stores, phone sales, and TV shopping.

### Required Pre-Purchase Disclosures
Before completing a distance sale, the seller must provide:
1. Full product/service description, including key characteristics
2. Total price including all taxes, fees, and charges
3. Payment terms and accepted methods
4. Delivery timeline and associated costs
5. Full cancellation and return policy
6. Seller's identity, address, and contact details
7. Warranty terms (if applicable)

## Cooling-Off Periods

### Standard Period (14 Days)
- Consumer may cancel a distance transaction within 14 days of receiving the product
- For services: within 14 days of the transaction date or receiving the contract, whichever is later
- Written cancellation notice required (email, fax, or mail accepted)

### Extended Period (4 Months)
The following groups receive an extended 4-month cooling-off period:
- People with disabilities (as defined by law)
- Senior citizens aged 65 and over
- New immigrants (olim chadashim) who have been in Israel less than 5 years

The seller must inform these consumers of their extended rights.

## Cancellation Fee Limits
- Maximum cancellation fee: 5% of the transaction price OR 100 NIS, whichever is **lower**
- Return shipping cost: borne by the buyer unless the item is defective
- If the item is defective or does not match the description: full refund, no cancellation fee, seller pays return shipping

## Refund Timeline
- Seller must issue refund within 14 days of receiving the cancellation notice
- Refund must be via the same payment method used for the original transaction
- Credit card refunds: seller must notify credit card company to cancel the charge

## Exceptions to Cancellation Right
The following product categories are exempt from the cooling-off period:
- Perishable goods (food, flowers, etc.)
- Products manufactured or customized to buyer's specifications
- Digital content that has been downloaded or accessed
- Hygiene products that have been opened (undergarments, swimwear, cosmetics)
- Products that cannot be returned due to health/hygiene regulations
- Accommodation, transportation, or leisure services for specific dates

## Price Display Requirements

### VAT Inclusion
- All consumer-facing prices must include 17% VAT (Ma'am)
- It is illegal to display pre-VAT prices to consumers
- Display format: "₪X.XX" or "X.XX ש"ח" — both are acceptable

### Delivery Costs
- Shipping/delivery costs must be clearly stated before the checkout process
- Hidden fees added at checkout violate the Consumer Protection Law
- Total order cost (including all fees) must be visible before payment confirmation

### Discounts and Sales
- Discounted items must show both the original price and the sale price
- "From" pricing (e.g., "from ₪99") is only permitted when the base price genuinely exists
- Sale period and conditions must be clearly stated

## Required Business Disclosures
E-commerce sellers must prominently display:
1. **Business name** — as registered with the Registrar of Companies (Rasham HaChevarot)
2. **Registration number** — Osek Murshe number or Company Number (C.N.)
3. **Physical address** — actual business address, not just a PO Box
4. **Phone number** — reachable during business hours
5. **Email address** — monitored and responsive
6. **Contact person** — full name of business owner or authorized representative

These details must appear on the website and on every invoice/receipt issued.

## IS 5568 Accessibility Standard (Summary)
- Based on WCAG 2.0 Level AA
- Applies to businesses with 25+ employees, public service providers, and government sites
- Enforced under Amendment 36 to the Equal Rights for People with Disabilities Law
- Key requirements: alt text, keyboard navigation, 4.5:1 color contrast, form labels, RTL support
- Accessibility statement page required
- Violations may result in lawsuits and fines

## Cookie Consent Requirements
Under the Communications Law (Amendment 2022):
- Non-essential cookies require explicit opt-in consent
- Essential cookies (session, authentication, cart) are exempt
- Consent banner must appear before non-essential cookies are set
- Users must be able to withdraw consent easily
- Cookie types and purposes must be clearly described
- Privacy policy must include cookie information
